Changing Thoughts on Teaching

June 21, 2016

When I first got my internship at UTeach, I was slightly worried. I hadn't really ever taught before, and I have a bit of a hard time relating to kids. While being trained for the position, I got the impression that I wouldn't be the one teaching the Arduino lessons, but instead I would be assisting an actual teacher.

The friday before camp started, I found out that I would be leading the Arduino lessons for 8 hours per day, 4 hours per class. The other intern I worked with did not know much about Arduino, so I was basically in charge.

A Summer of Creation

May 21, 2016

Every summer of my life thus far has been unproductive. Every fall I return to school having lost much of the academic progress that I made in the spring. This summer I will be spending it away from home for the most part. I will spend my time in Austin, where I will be working for UTeach, a summer camp for middle and high school students. I'm going to be teaching kids the basics of the Arduino microcontroller, as well as assisting a teacher with an Engineering class. Aside from that, I will be working on TruRecruit, graphic design, HackTX, and whatever other creative project I come up with. Its time for me to make positive change.

This will be a summer of growth and creation.

Learning Back End for TruRecruit

April 26, 2016

The journey of TruRecruit started in February 2016 during uMAD, which is an all day developer's conference on UT's campus. I ran into Jesse, who I had met at HackTX 2015. He told me of the idea of TruRecruit, and I said I was on board pretty quickly. Shortly after, we discussed it with Bongani, who also jumped on board quickly. During the next few weeks, we slowly started to work on our web app. As a start, we found and used a Hackathon Starter by Sahat as a base. This worked great because it was essentially a big MEAN stack template that had user authentication built-in using OAuth2.

Coming into TruRecruit, my experience in backend web development consisted of only a few node and mongodb tutorials. Luckily for me, I learn best under pressure, so I did my best to learn the MEAN stack for TruRecruit. While researching the best way for us to upload resume's, I came across AWS S3. This solution was so much better than the local storage idea I thought we would use. While trying to implement S3 with Node, I read various developer articles on the increased performance of using Golang as a backend language. Specifically, I learned that handling hundreds of requests in a short time (like TruRecruit will hopefully do) to a service like S3 would be better done with Go as a backend language. Currently, I am implementing our landing page and login/signup pages in the Go web framework Revel. I hope to have authentication soon, with file upload in S3 to follow.
